# Usability Evaluation – Stress & Mood Tracker

## Tasks
1. Log today’s mood and stress.
2. Add at least one contextual tag (sleep, study, social, or exercise).
3. Open the dashboard and identify:
   - the highest stress day
   - the average mood.

## Questions
1. What was confusing or unclear?
2. What felt easy or intuitive?
3. What would you change or improve?
4. Would you use this daily? Why or why not?

---

## Participants
5 university students (anonymous)

---

## Results

### Participant 1
- Confusing: Difficulty locating the dashboard.
- Easy: Mood and stress sliders were intuitive.
- Improvement: Suggested adding a reward system.
- Daily use: Yes, if motivational features were added.

### Participant 2
- Confusing: No major issues reported.
- Easy: Data logging was straightforward.
- Improvement: Suggested replacing numeric mood values with descriptive labels.
- Daily use: Yes.

### Participant 3
- Confusing: Difficulty interpreting the graph.
- Easy: Tagging was simple.
- Improvement: Preferred a calmer or less technical visualization.
- Daily use: Yes.

### Participant 4
- Confusing: No confusion reported.
- Easy: Tags were clear and direct.
- Improvement: Requested a weekly summary view.
- Daily use: No.

### Participant 5
- Confusing: No major issues reported.
- Easy: Interface was straightforward.
- Improvement: Suggested a reward system and additional tags.
- Daily use: Yes.


---

## Summary of Findings
- Several participants had difficulty locating or interpreting the dashboard and graphs.
- Mood values presented as numbers were not always meaningful to users.
- Participants expressed a desire for motivational elements such as rewards or feedback.
- Users wanted higher-level summaries (e.g., weekly overview) instead of only raw graphs.


## Changes Made After Evaluation
- Renamed the dashboard tab to improve discoverability.
- Added a streak-based reward system to increase user motivation and engagement.
- Added a confirmation message after saving entries to improve user feedback.
- Planned future improvements such as descriptive mood labels and weekly summaries.

---

## Limitations
- The evaluation involved a small number of participants.
- Feedback was qualitative and self-reported.
- The system is intended for reflection only and does not provide medical advice.
